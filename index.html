    I am a third-year Computer Science student at Nantes University with a strong interest in designing creative and technically complex systems. I am particularly drawn to projects that combine analytical thinking with creative problem-solving, allowing me to design architectures, simulate environments, and build meaningful technological solutions. My primary current project is the development of a graph-based risk engine using Python, NumPy, NetworkX, PyVis, and Pandas. The objective is to identify fraudulent transactions within a simulated financial environment by modelling relationships and risk propagation across a network. The initial implementation focuses on a deterministic, non-AI approach to establish a solid analytical foundation. I then plan to extend the system by integrating recursive agent-based decision loops to increase adaptability and detection capability. In parallel, I am working on training basic Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) and Decision Trees on the CIFAR-10 dataset. This project explores model performance under constrained conditions, including the use of Canny edge filters and extremely reduced image resolutions (Tiny format, 8×8). The goal is to better understand model behaviour, feature extraction, and performance trade-offs in limited-information environments. Among my completed projects, I designed and implemented a fully functioning compiler in C++ using Flex, Bison, Yacc, and XML. This compiler translates a custom pseudocode-style algorithmic language into executable C++ code, demonstrating my understanding of lexical analysis, parsing, syntax trees, and code generation. A link to this project is provided below. I have also completed an advanced database engineering project involving the design of a prison management system. This project focused on applying advanced database concepts including Boyce-Codd Normal Form, query optimization, indexing strategies, and views to ensure efficiency, integrity, and scalability. The work was accompanied by extensive documentation and schema design. Additionally, I have collaborated on several team-based software engineering projects. During my second year, I contributed to the development of a Monopoly-style game in Java, implementing core gameplay logic and object-oriented design principles. I was also part of a larger team of twenty contributors working on the development of a farm simulation game using Spring Boot, where I gained experience working within a structured collaborative development environment.
